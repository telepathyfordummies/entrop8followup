Follow up on Entropy

In the previous laboratory exercise, entropy was observed through the mixing of particles within a closed system. While the process appeared straightforward, an important question remains: why do systems tend to evolve toward these mixed states?

The answer lies in probability.

Every observable condition of a system (macrostate) can be produced by one or more of the microscopic arrangements (microstates). While all microstates are considered equally likely, some macrostates can be produced by vastly more microstates than others.

As a result, high entropy macrostates are statistically favored because there are simply more ways for them to occur.

In this folllow up, you will compare the probabilities of different macrostates and investigate the statistical foundation of entropy.
